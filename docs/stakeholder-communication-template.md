## Stakeholder Communication Best Practices

### Frequency Guidelines
- **Weekly:** For high-priority or at-risk projects
- **Bi-weekly:** For active projects in execution phase
- **Monthly:** For stable projects or long-term initiatives
- **Ad-hoc:** For critical issues, scope changes, or escalations

### Audience Tailoring
- **Executive Leadership:** Focus on executive summary, health dashboard, and decisions needed
- **Product Team:** Emphasize progress, metrics, and upcoming priorities
- **Engineering Teams:** Highlight dependencies, technical risks, and resource needs
- **Cross-functional Partners:** Focus on dependencies and collaboration points

### Communication Channels
- **Email:** For formal updates and record-keeping
- **Slack/Teams:** For quick status and informal updates
- **Meetings:** For complex discussions or decision-making
- **Dashboard/Portal:** For self-service real-time status

### Writing Tips
- **Be Concise:** Use bullet points and tables for scannability
- **Be Honest:** Don't hide problems; escalate early
- **Be Visual:** Use status indicators (ğŸŸ¢ğŸŸ¡ğŸ”´) and trend arrows (â†—ï¸â¡ï¸â†˜ï¸)
- **Be Actionable:** Clearly state what you need from stakeholders
- **Be Consistent:** Use same format and schedule for predictability

### Distribution Strategy
- **To:** Primary stakeholders and decision-makers
- **CC:** Extended team members and interested parties
- **BCC:** (Generally avoid; use distribution lists instead)
- **Archive:** Store in shared location accessible to all team members

## Specialized Communication Templates

### For Milestone Communications
Include milestone significance, customer impact, and next phase preview.

### For Scope Change Communications
Clearly document original vs. new scope, rationale, impact analysis, and revised timeline/budget.

### For Crisis/Incident Communications
Follow incident communication template in [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md).

### For Project Kickoff Communications
Introduce team, clarify roles, set expectations, and establish communication cadence.

### For Project Closure Communications
Summarize achievements, thank participants, document learnings, and outline transition plan.
